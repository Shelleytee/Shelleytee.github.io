Responsive Sites and Why Their Important
Studies show that theS majority of users do most of their browsing via their phone or hand held device. So having a website that looks good and is easily navigated regardless of what device it is being viewed on is the difference between being seen and not being seen.

Mobile First Design and Why It's Important
Planning you design around mobile first means that you prioritse the smallest screen size in your design look and then design up to the largest screen size. This ensures that the website will be viewed optiminally from the smallest screen to the largest.

Frameworks - The Good, The Bad, and The Ugly
Frameworks are a fast and simply way for developers to speed up the coding processes. They are a template of sample code snippets most commonly used by the majority of web developers. Frameworks also help to promote the usage of consistent terminology, good coding practice, and dynamic updating functionality. Unfortunately, they are also weighted down with lots of code snippets a developer may not use in every website, they require a learning curve by the developer and are not necessarily that well detailed to explain all their elements, and as a result of having all that extra code they can be quite big in file size.

Wireframes - Comics for Stickmen
Wireframes are a simple collection of design layouts for user screens that have no colour, text, or image detail in them. They are basic outlined placeholders on a page. These help a developer to quickly draw out a layout idea to see how things will work. And, they are a great to just pop infront of a client to confirm basic understanding before investing lots of hours into a pretty layout that does not meet a clients needs.
