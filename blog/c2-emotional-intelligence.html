Captivating thoughts from someone who tries not to think.

11 December 2016

“The ability to monitor one’s own and others’ feelings and emotions, to discriminate among them and to use this information to guide one’s thinking and actions”

Excerpt From: Tan, Chade-Meng. “Search Inside Yourself: The Unexpected Path to Achieving Success, Happiness (and World Peace).” iBooks.

Seach Inside Yourself - Chapter One Learnings

The following is a summary of my takeaway from chapter one - even an engineer can thrive on emotional intelligence.

  • Emotional intelligence (IE) is learned competencies
  • The 5 Domains are:
    • Self Awareness - knowledge of one's internal states
    • Self Regulation - management of one's internal states
    • Motivation - emotional tendencies that guide you to your goals
    • Empathy - awareness of others' feelings, needs, concerns
    • Social Skills - adeptness at inducing desirable responses in others
  • IE enables stellar work performance, outstanding leadership, and the ability to create conditions for happiness
  • Learn IE by:
    • Paying attention
    • Listen to your body
  • Start with two minutes mindfullness a day

Mindfulness Feedback

“Paying attention in a particular way: on purpose, in the present moment, and non-judgmentally.” - Jon Kabat-Zinn.

  • The Easy Way - just sit for two minutes and focus on breath. This was very calming and I want to say I will continue this practice from this point onwards.
  • The Easier Way - sit without agenda for two minutes. I also tried this and found it to be also very calming.
  • Mix and Match - whilst in mindfulness switch between the easy way and the easier way. I did not try this but will use it in times where my mind is easily distracted. Two minutes can take a long time when you can't sit still.
