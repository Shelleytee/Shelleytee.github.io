Mindfulness and Self Awareness
Mindfulness is the voluntary action of the dualistic mind to observe the responses to experiences and phenomena. Awareness is what is revealed to mind through the union of an intellectual foundation’s influence on the meditative state. It is the result of a series of revelations to and experiences of the non-dualistic mind.

What is the difference between mindfuless and awareness? How to develop both? - Harry C Emberson 18/10/16

Clarity & Presence... Self-awareness is about clarity, and mindfulness is about being present.

Self-awareness competencies are:

  1. Emotional awareness
    • Recognising one's emotions and their effects.
  2. Accurate self-assessment
    • Knowing one's strengths and limits.
  3. Self-confidence
    • A strong sense of one;s self-worth and capabilities.

Mindfulness and self-awareness are so very close in meaning that they fit together as part of a EQ skillset that leads to self-confidence.

Self-Awareness Techniques
  • Body Scan
    • Settling attention - sit comfortably for 2 minutes.
    • Scan body - starting with the head, face, nech and shoulder, back, front, then entire body all at once.
    • Scan for emotion - did you find an emotion in your body? If so, notice its presence, else, notice the absence of emotions, and catch one if it arises in teh next two minutes.
    • Return to grounding - now return to the present, bringing your attention back to your body, then your breath.
  • Journaling
    • By just taking four minutes to jot down your thoughts about, how you are feeling right now? Are you aware that...? What motivates you is...? What are you inspired by...? Today, What do you aspire to? What hurts you...? What do you wish...? How others are...? What happy mistake...? Your thoughts on love...?
    • Or two minutes for to note the things that give you pleasure are...? And your stregths are...?

Personally I found the body scan a helpful tool in developing my self-awareness. With sprint 5 being very challenging, this helped me to stablise inbetween stress moments. And was beneficial in helping me to identify the my use of chocolate as a cruch was not helping me. I have now transitioned to chewing gum.

My Emotions Are Not Me... I Hope

This is a very big concept and took a bit of thinking, analysing, acknowledging, and adjusting to grasp. It is so easy to define ourselves by how we feel instead of who we are, and that how we feel is simply a moment in time of who we are that I forget that whilst I am both I am not my emotions but me.
