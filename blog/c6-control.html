Personal Triggers

Some of the triggers that result in an emotional response for me during this huge coding learning curve are road blocks. When I just can't see the logic and the pathe the code needs to follow I melt. It feels like my brain goes into possum mode. It goes blank and just stares at the oncoming headlights. I experience shallow breathing, flight or flight, have difficulty paying attention. As well as, the ongoing reading. There is so much reading and I am a watching, talking learner. The volume of reading triggers a non-responsive, non-absorption mode. I can read the same sentence 200 times and I still don't know what it is saying. When this happens I get stressed, a mild headache, sore eyes, and sleepy.

Siberian North Railroad Techniques/Exercise
  1. STOP
    • When you feel triggered just stop. The 'sacred pause', do this one if nothing else.
  2. BREATHE
    • By focusing the mind on the breath we reinforce the 'sacred pause'.
  3. NOTICE
    • Experience your emotion by bringing attention to your body.
  4. REFLECT
    • Where is the emotion coming from? Think about past, present, patterns
  5. RESPOND
    • Finally, bring to mind ways in which you might respond to the situation that would have a positive outcome.

From a personal perspective when I tried this exercise I found it initially difficult to simply STOP. Or really to register that moment when I'm actually at the beginning of the trigger. Just this bit took time to acknowledge. Then once I went through the steps I found it calming. Gave me a little more focus to continue, and encouraged me to bring myself back to my center.
