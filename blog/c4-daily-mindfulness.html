Butt on Cushion... If Only

Captivating thoughts from someone who tries not to think.
16 January 2017
“And the faculty of voluntarily bringing back a wandering attention over and over again is the very root of judgment, character, and will. No one is compos sui (master of himself) if he have it not. An education which should improve this faculty would be the education par excellence.”

Excerpt From: Tan, Chade-Meng. “Search Inside Yourself: The Unexpected Path to Achieving Success, Happiness (and World Peace).” iBooks.

A Mindfulness Conversation

The follow techniques were recommended by Gary Friedman are:

  1. Mindful Listening
    • Listening to someone for 3-4 consecutive minutes. Refrain from interrupting, just listen.
  2. Looping
    • Then repeat back in an abbreviated way what you have just heard to show comprehension and understanding.
  3. Dipping
    • Finally, check in with ourselves, if distracted, bring your attention back to the other person. Acknowledge your distraction then return.

This was interesting. I used this to listen attentively to my daughter. I usually zone out. She makes me very tired when she talks to me. But this time I only got a little bit tired, I think I will stick with my active listening skills with this particular dynamic.

I also used this a couple of times and found it interesting how I was able to stay listening, though not easily. My first attempt was not very good and I zoned out about the one minute mark. However, with ongoing effort I was able to sustain the three minute goal and was rewarded by the person I was listening to feeling completely engaged with me. Reflecting back their words to confirm what I'd heard created a more bonded and personal moment in the conversation.

Techniques to Sustain Me

The follow techniques were recommended by Gary Friedman are:

  1. Have a buddy
    • Find a meditation buddy. Most people find it easier commiting to their mediation practice when they do it with a friend.
  2. Do less than you can
    • If you can sit in mindfulness for five minutes before it feels like a choe, then DO NOT sit for five minutes, just do three or four minutes.
  3. Take one breath a day
    • Finally, Breathe in, and breath out... And your commitment for the day is fulfilled; everything else is a bonus.

But what is the sustained practice for me you ask? Here, here it is in the "Take one breath a day". This is how I will sustain my practice. Wait you say, how can it be that simple. Look above, the book said so...
